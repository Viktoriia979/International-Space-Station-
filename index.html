International Space Station (ISS):

The International Space Station (ISS) is used for scientific research and international cooperation. Astronauts from different countries live and work on the ISS while carrying out experiments that cannot be done on Earth because of microgravity. The station is used to study human health, plants, animals, and new technology. Scientists use the results to improve life on Earth and to prepare for future space missions to the Moon and Mars.

The ISS is the largest human-made satellite ever built. It is about 109 metres long, which is slightly longer than a football field, and weighs about 420,000 kilograms. The first part of the station was launched into space in 1998, and more sections were added over several years. It is made from strong but lightweight materials, including aluminium alloys, titanium, and stainless steel. It also has large solar panels that collect sunlight to produce electricity for the station.

The International Space Station does not travel to other planets. Instead, it orbits the Earth in low Earth orbit, about 400 kilometres above the Earth's surface. It travels at a speed of about 28,000 kilometres per hour, completing one orbit every 90 minutes. This means the astronauts on board see about 16 sunrises and sunsets every day while the ISS circles the Earth.
